# Learning and Predictive Coding

The notes here converge on the idea that cortex implements prediction and prediction-error computations tightly coupled with reward-based learning.

[[brain-vs-ai-world-models]] proposes that neocortex and cerebellum both function as general-purpose world models, unifying prediction, understanding, and generation under one architecture — paralleling how Transformers implement diverse tasks with a single backbone. It integrates internal model theory, predictive coding, and mirror neurons.

[[cell-type-prediction-and-novelty]] grounds the normative story in cortical microcircuits: a model assigning excitatory neurons to prediction-error encoding, VIP to reward/surprise, and SST to maintaining predictions reproduces Allen Visual Behavior novelty data. The VIP→SST disinhibitory motif is critical for balancing energy efficiency against representational capacity.

[[latent-knowledge-sensory-cortex]] provides striking in vivo evidence that auditory cortex carries separable higher-order teaching signals — a reward-prediction ensemble and an action-suppression ensemble — that emerge within tens of trials and causally drive associative learning. Crucially, there is *no* cortical map expansion; AC tutors subcortical structures and becomes dispensable at expert level.

Together these notes argue that sensory cortex is better described as a "sensory-enriched associative cortex" that hosts rapid, cell-type-specific predictive and reward-related computations rather than merely refining cue representations.
