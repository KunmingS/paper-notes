# Visual Cortex and Coding

Classical V1 was described by linear Gabor receptive fields and a simple/complex cell dichotomy. The notes in this cluster show that neither description survives contact with modern tools.

[[inception-loop-bipartite-invariance]] uses CNN digital twins and closed-loop experiments to synthesize Most Exciting Inputs (Walker et al. 2019) and Varied Exciting Inputs (Ding, Tran et al. 2026). MEIs turn out to be complex nonlinear features — sharp corners, pointillist textures — and VEIs reveal that most V1 neurons have a bipartite receptive field: a fixed low-frequency subfield plus a shift-invariant high-frequency texture subfield, aligned with natural object–background boundaries. MICrONS connectomics shows invariance building up across synaptic stages.

[[stimulus-driven-v1-dimensionality]] takes the population view: macaque V1 population responses live in a surprisingly low-dimensional subspace whose dimensionality tracks stimulus complexity (gratings < natural < noise), and different stimuli largely reuse a shared repertoire of basis patterns. Deep CNN analysis predicts that natural stimuli should gain relative dimensionality along the ventral hierarchy.

These results connect directly to the [[wiki/population-geometry-and-manifolds]] framework and to [[wiki/thalamus-and-subcortical]], since LGN pre-processing and corticothalamic feedback shape the very responses V1 digital twins model.
