# Thalamus and Subcortical Processing

[[thalamus-visual-function-review]] makes the case that the visual thalamus is anything but a passive relay. The LGN receives only 5–10% of its synapses from the retina; the remaining 90–95% come from cortical layer 6, the thalamic reticular nucleus, brainstem neuromodulatory systems, and local interneurons. Parallel M/P/K streams in primates (and X/Y/W in cat) are matched by stream-specific corticogeniculate feedback from V1, V2, and mid-level extrastriate areas, which sharpens spatial resolution and temporal precision.

Local LGN interneurons use bifunctional dendrites and F2 triadic terminals for fast feedforward inhibition. Burst vs. tonic firing and neuromodulation implement behavioral-state and attentional gating. The pulvinar forms transthalamic cortico-thalamo-cortical loops that coordinate inter-areal cortical communication and attention. Sherman & Guillery's driver-modulator framework distinguishes first-order sensory relays from higher-order transthalamic relays carrying predictive signals.

This reframing connects directly to [[wiki/visual-cortex-and-coding]] (V1 responses reflect already-transformed signals) and to [[wiki/neuroai-and-embodiment]] — the fact that feedback dominates even the "most feedforward" sensory pathway is exactly the point [[embodied-neuroai-musculoskeletal]] makes about why disembodied feedforward models of perception are incomplete.
