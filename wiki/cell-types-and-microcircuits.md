# Cell Types and Microcircuits

Cortical computation is ultimately implemented by identifiable cell types wired into canonical motifs. Two notes here make that concrete from complementary angles.

[[human-l2-3-pyramidal-subtypes]] is an unusually large-scale multi-patch study of human temporal cortex (1,400+ neurons, 1,400+ monosynaptic connections from 23 epilepsy patients). It identifies four electrophysiological subtypes of L2-3 pyramidal neurons — SlowAP, FastAP, DoubSpk, and LowRin — with subtype-specific wiring rules: LowRin neurons act as convergence hubs, SlowAP forms sparse strong depressing synapses, FastAP→LowRin is asymmetric, and these principles are conserved across patients. Notably, electrophysiological classification captures functional dimensions that transcriptomic types miss, implying post-translational regulation matters for computation.

[[cell-type-prediction-and-novelty]] takes the computational side: a microcircuit model assigns specific algorithmic roles to excitatory, VIP, and SST neurons during novelty detection, and ablation studies show the VIP→SST disinhibitory motif is essential for balancing efficiency and capacity. The model reproduces Allen Visual Behavior data.

Read together, these notes illustrate the two halves of circuit neuroscience: empirical identification of functional subtypes and their wiring (Planert et al.), and normative/mechanistic modeling of what each subtype computes (Sharafeldin & Choi). They connect to [[wiki/learning-and-predictive-coding]] and to the broader manifold-to-circuit program in [[unifying-manifolds-and-circuits]].
