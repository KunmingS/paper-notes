# Breaking the Jar: Why NeuroAI Needs Embodiment

## TL;DR
Perspective arguing that NeuroAI must model brains together with bodies. Three pillars — feedback, biomechanics, modularity — explain why disembodied models miss how nervous systems actually compute.

## Key claims
- Feedback dominates even "feedforward" pathways: most LGN inputs are non-retinal.
- Biomechanics offloads computation (the dead-trout-in-turbulence example); the brain controls muscles/forces, not joint positions.
- Modular bottlenecks (RGCs, motoneurons) let researchers compose models at different resolutions.
- Tools (MuJoCo, MJX, full mouse/rat/fly musculoskeletal models, Drosophila connectome) now make embodied modeling tractable.
- Foundation models trained purely on neural/video data risk bypassing the causal structure of embodied control.

## Why it matters
Reframes NeuroAI priorities toward whole-animal, sensorimotor, muscle-level models; motivates Zador's embodied Turing test and the 2024 NIH BRAIN NeuroAI agenda.
