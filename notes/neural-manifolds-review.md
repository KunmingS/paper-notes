# Neural Manifolds Review

## TL;DR
Thematic review of the neural manifold framework: population activity occupies low-dimensional subspaces whose geometry, topology, and dynamics reflect circuit structure and computation across motor, sensory, cognitive, and navigation systems.

## Key claims
- Population doctrine: dimensionality reduction (PCA, GPFA, dPCA, LFADS, CEBRA, MARBLE, DFINE) is the standard first step; high-dimensional single-neuron activity and low-dimensional latent dynamics are compatible.
- Neural population geometry (Chung/Abbott) quantifies manifold capacity; untangling along hierarchies increases linear separability.
- Topological data analysis revealed the toroidal manifold of grid cells and ring of head-direction cells — direct evidence for continuous attractor models.
- Motor cortex shows rotational dynamics and manifolds preserved across tasks and over weeks.
- Low-rank RNN theory ties manifolds to connectivity; manifold alignment enables stable BMIs across sessions and individuals.

## Why it matters
Provides the shared geometric vocabulary for linking recordings to circuit mechanisms across systems, and motivates the push to unify manifold descriptions with causal circuit models.
