# Decoding the Brain: From Representations to Mechanistic Models

## TL;DR
Cell perspective framing brain computation as a cascade of encoding-decoding operations. Reviews linear, Bayesian, and deep-learning decoders and argues for a shift from descriptive models to causal, mechanistic ones that integrate normative and data-driven approaches.

## Key claims
- Encoding P(K|x) and decoding P(x|K) are two sides of the same cascade; hierarchies transform implicit into explicit representations (V1→IT).
- Linear decoders (population vectors), k-NN, and Bayesian/Kalman decoders span biologically plausible to statistically powerful readouts; Cramér-Rao bounds quality.
- Fully observed GLMs/copulas vs. latent variable models; large-scale recordings now enable per-session deep models.
- Dopamine RPE circuit illustrates encoding-decoding at mechanistic level (VTA DA, VTA GABA, upstream partial RPEs).
- Data-driven models need to be fused with normative frameworks to achieve causal understanding.

## Why it matters
A clean conceptual and mathematical bridge between classical population-coding neuroscience and modern BCI/deep-learning practice, pushing toward causal mechanistic models.
