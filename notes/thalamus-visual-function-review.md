# Thalamus in Visual Function: Active Processor, Not Passive Relay

## TL;DR
Thematic review arguing the visual thalamus is an active computational hub: LGN receives only 5–10% retinal input, parallel M/P/K streams are modulated by layer-6 feedback, TRN, and neuromodulation, and pulvinar coordinates cortical communication.

## Key claims
- LGN is dominated by non-retinal inputs — cortical feedback outnumbers retinal synapses ~10:1.
- Parallel M/P/K (primate) and X/Y/W (cat) streams are maintained and matched by stream-specific corticogeniculate feedback from V1, V2, and mid-level extrastriate areas.
- LGN interneurons with bifunctional dendrites (F2 terminals) provide fast feedforward inhibition; corticogeniculate feedback sharpens temporal precision and spatial resolution.
- TRN and burst/tonic firing implement state- and attention-dependent gating.
- Pulvinar forms transthalamic cortico-thalamo-cortical loops and coordinates inter-areal attentional communication.
- Driver-modulator framework distinguishes first-order (sensory) from higher-order (transthalamic) relays; predictive processing signals are present in thalamus.

## Why it matters
Overturns the textbook "relay station" view and aligns thalamic function with embodied, feedback-dominated, predictive-processing accounts of perception.
