# Unifying Neural Manifolds and Circuits for Cognition

## TL;DR
Nat Rev Neurosci perspective arguing that manifold and circuit frameworks are inseparable. Low-rank connectivity generates low-dimensional manifolds in heterogeneous populations, and latent-circuit inference extracts causal circuit mechanisms from data.

## Key claims
- Manifold descriptions are method-dependent and purely descriptive without circuit grounding; circuits alone cannot explain distributed mixed selectivity.
- Low-rank connectivity (rank-one outer products) constrains dynamics to low-dimensional manifolds even in heterogeneous networks.
- Latent-circuit framework jointly fits embedding Q and low-dimensional dynamics, enabling causal perturbation predictions.
- Fly head direction system is the gold standard: 20-year-old ring attractor predictions confirmed at single-cell resolution via EM and RNA profiling.
- Grid cell torus is established at the manifold level; circuit confirmation in mammals pending.
- Latent-circuit and demixed-regression analyses of context-dependent decision RNNs give contradictory conclusions; only latent-circuit predictions survive causal testing.

## Why it matters
Sets the agenda for bridging geometric/dynamical descriptions with mechanistic circuit models — essential for causal and clinically relevant understanding of cognition.
