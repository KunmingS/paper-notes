# Inception Loops and Bipartite Invariance in Mouse V1

## TL;DR
Closed-loop record-model-generate-verify paradigms move V1 receptive-field characterization beyond Gabors. Walker et al. 2019 found complex nonlinear MEIs; Ding, Tran et al. 2026 extend to VEIs and discover a bipartite fixed-plus-shift-invariant RF aligned with figure-ground boundaries.

## Key claims
- MEIs from CNN digital twins outperform Gabors and natural images; most V1 MEIs show sharp corners, pointillist textures, not Gabors.
- VEIs (20 dissimilar but strongly activating stimuli) reveal that most V1 neurons are neither simple nor complex cells.
- Bipartite RF structure: a fixed low-frequency subfield plus a shift-invariant high-frequency texture subfield.
- 99.1% of V1 neurons prefer stimuli containing boundaries — suggests role in figure-ground segmentation.
- MICrONS analysis: invariance increases along synaptic hierarchy; low-invariance neurons are building blocks, like-to-like connectivity.

## Why it matters
Redefines the classical simple/complex cell dichotomy and shows how digital twins plus connectomics can connect single-neuron tuning to circuit-level computation.
