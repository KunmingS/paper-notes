# Brain vs AI: World-Model-Based Versatile Circuit Computation

## TL;DR
Ohmae & Ohmae (arXiv 2024) argue that both neocortex and cerebellum function as general-purpose world models whose versatility mirrors modern AI. A three-element decomposition (circuit structure, I/O, learning algorithm) enables broad brain-AI comparison beyond the usual "CNN ≈ visual cortex" analogy.

## Key claims
- Neocortex and cerebellum use uniform circuitry yet support diverse functions, just as Transformers do in AI.
- Both brains and AI implement prediction and prediction-error learning as a core principle.
- Three cognitive processes unify cortical/cerebellar function: prediction, understanding (compressed internal models), and generation (reusing prediction for motor, imagination, language).
- Integrates internal model theory (Wolpert/Kawato), predictive coding, and mirror neurons under one world-model umbrella.

## Why it matters
Reframes cortical and cerebellar computation as a single general-purpose architecture akin to foundation models, bridging theoretical neuroscience and modern AI.
