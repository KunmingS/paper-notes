# Science AI agents and SaaS landscape (2026 snapshot)

## TL;DR
Snapshot of agent-shaped AI products in scientific research as of early 2026. **Elicit** dominates the literature/synthesis layer (138M+ papers, Claude Opus backend, freemium-to-enterprise funnel). **Kosmos** (Edison Scientific) is the most aggressive autonomous-scientist play — claims 7 independent discoveries including a novel neuronal senescence mechanism. **Lila Sciences** is building toward autonomous wet labs. Drug discovery (Formation Bio, Recursion, Insilico Medicine) is the hottest vertical. Most products live in the **"dry" loop** — literature, data extraction, hypothesis generation. Closed-loop "design experiment → execute → analyze" autonomous science is not yet a real product category.

## Key players
- **Elicit** — structured systematic review, data extraction, report generation. Not a chatbot — provides tables, multi-step workflows. Already adopted by pharma for clinical-trial literature analysis. Built on Claude Opus.
- **Kosmos** (Edison Scientific) — "AI scientist" that parallelizes hundreds of research tasks, compressing months into a day. Technical report: 4 of 7 completed discoveries are novel, including identifying a new neuronal senescence mechanism.
- **Lila Sciences** — "scientific superintelligence" via autonomous labs — scaling the scientific method itself.
- **Formation Bio, Recursion, Insilico Medicine** — AI agents for drug-target discovery, molecular design, clinical data analysis.

## Business model
- **Tiered subscription** (Elicit: free 5K credits → $12/mo Plus → $49/mo Pro → $79/seat/mo Team). Team LTV is 3–4x individual.
- **Enterprise licensing** is where the real money is — pharma/biotech/consulting on annual contracts at much higher per-seat prices.
- **API access** — Elicit exposes paper-search and report-generation as B2B2C infrastructure.
- **Cost structure**: ~30% revenue on AI compute/cloud, ~20% R&D, ~15% sales/marketing. Foundation-model calls (Claude Opus etc.) compress gross margins well below classical SaaS.
- **Honest constraint**: the pure academic market is too small and price-insensitive to sustain large-scale science AI. Real growth comes from pharma, biotech, and policy consulting — not from individual researchers.

## Why it matters
A practical map of where AI is actually getting paid in science right now. Useful for calibrating "AI will transform research" claims: in 2026 the answer is "yes, but mostly in pharma and in literature workflows; closed-loop wet-science automation is still early."
