# Rapid Latent Knowledge in Auditory Cortex Drives Learning

## TL;DR
Mouse auditory cortex (AC) encodes rapid reward-prediction and action-suppression signals within tens of trials. AC tutors subcortical learning during acquisition but becomes dispensable at expert level — without any tonotopic map expansion.

## Key claims
- Task knowledge (probe trials) emerges in tens of trials while performance improves over thousands; reinforcement masks latent knowledge.
- Tensor decomposition identifies six dynamics; cell ensemble 5 encodes reward prediction, ensemble 6 encodes action suppression.
- Reward-prediction activity is not sensory, motor, or reward-consumption; silencing it post-trial impairs learning (teaching signal).
- Action-suppression activity drops at first lick on FA trials and causally gates licking on S- trials.
- No cortical map expansion accompanies learning; higher-order ensembles are spatially clustered but uncoupled from tonotopy.
- AC silencing impairs learning during acquisition but not after expertise — AC tutors subcortical circuits.

## Why it matters
Reframes sensory cortex as a "sensory-enriched associative cortex" carrying separable causal teaching signals, distinct from classical cue-plasticity models of learning.
