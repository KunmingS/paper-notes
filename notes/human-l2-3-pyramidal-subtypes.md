# Human L2-3 Pyramidal Neuron Electrophysiological Subtypes

## TL;DR
Massive multi-patch study of human temporal cortex identifies four electrophysiological subtypes of L2-3 pyramidal neurons (SlowAP, FastAP, DoubSpk, LowRin) with subtype-specific synaptic wiring rules conserved across patients.

## Key claims
- 1,400+ neurons and 1,400+ monosynaptic connections from 23 epilepsy patients support four robust e-types.
- Synaptic amplitudes are log-normal; connection probability, strength, PPR, and kinetics depend on pre/post e-type pair.
- LowRin neurons are convergence hubs; SlowAP forms sparse strong depressing synapses; FastAP→LowRin is asymmetric.
- E-type to transcriptomic type correspondence is limited — function isn't fully predicted by gene expression.
- Microcircuit wiring principles are conserved across individuals.

## Methods
- Simultaneous multi-neuron whole-cell patch clamp (up to 10 cells), 25 electrophysiological features, hierarchical clustering, biocytin morphology.

## Why it matters
Shows that the expanded human L2-3 microcircuit has fine-grained subtype-specific connectivity rules, constraining how cortical computation is implemented in humans.
